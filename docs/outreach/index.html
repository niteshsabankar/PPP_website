As part of our NSF-ABI's outreach program, we organized a workshop on introducing bioinformatics, genomics, and basics of programming for highschool teachers, in collaboration with TUTeach (https://cst.temple.edu/tuteach). The first version of our week-long workshop was successfully launched in Summer 2017, with 8 groups of participants working on collaboratively creating workable lesson plans for their highschool classrooms.

Through days 1-4, instructors (Dr. Jody Hey, Dr. Arun Sethuraman, and Dr. Andrew Webb) presented introductory/hands-on material on (1) programming (Python) basics, (2) genomic ancestry, (3) using genomics to inform medicine, and (4) the scope/future of computer programming in biology. All these lessons/material are now available here (organized by days).

Day 1: Introduction

On Day 1, Drs. Hey, Sethuraman, and Webb presented material on introducing their own work, Python programming concepts, and allowed time for a hands-on activity for teachers to write a simple Python program that takes in a DNA sequence as input, and outputs the reverse complement of the sequence.

  • Bioinformatics workshop
  • Introduction to python

Day 2: Genomics and Ancestry

On Day 2, Dr. Sethuraman introduced his research in genomic ancestry, methods for estimating ancestry, and went through a hands-on activity for computing genetic ancestry using M&M candies (as placeholders for genes/alleles).

  • Ancestry
  • M&M activity

Day 3: Genomic Medicine

Day 3 was Dr. Webb's introduction to the applications of the fields of bioinformatics and genomics to medicine, including studies of Genome-Wide Association (GWAS), and quantitative genetics.

  • Genomics disease overview
  • Classroom activity on GWAS

Day 4: Advanced Python, Future

Day 4, being the last day of instruction, we provided teachers with additional programming resources, as well as helped facilitate a discussion on the future, and scope of incorporating compuational skills in a highschool biology classroom.

  • Computer basics
  • Some more python

Day 5: Lesson Plans

On Day 5, teachers worked in groups to come up with lesson plans that could potentially be incorporated into their own classrooms, towards the goal of inculcating programming habits in a highschool classroom. 8 groups presented their lesson plans at the end of the day, which can be accessed here.
